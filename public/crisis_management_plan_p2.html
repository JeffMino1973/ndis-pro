🚨 1. Immediate Crisis Protocol
If your child is currently being violent, destroying property, or placing anyone in danger:
Operator Script (What to Say)
'My child has a disability and is currently experiencing a severe behavioral and mental health crisis. We are unsafe. Please dispatch police alongside an ambulance immediately so healthcare workers are present.'
↩️ Immediate Safety Check: If threatened, step away immediately to a safe, lockable room, outside, or a trusted neighbour's house. Do not try to reason or argue during active escalation.
📈 2. Support Services & Care Options
NSW Mental Health Line (1800 011 511)
A 24/7 statewide triage network staffed by trained clinicians. They can initiate local Crisis Assessment and Treatment Teams (CATT) or coordinate safe admission routes with local hospitals.
1800RESPECT (1800 737 732)
Confidential 24/7 crisis counselling and domestic safety planning. They support family members facing violence from adult dependants.
Carer Gateway (1800 422 737)
Available Monday to Friday, 8 AM – 5 PM. Contact them to arrange emergency respite care to physically step in and provide a safe transition when circumstances become unmanageable.
🔄 3. Next Steps & NDIS Safeguarding
Once the immediate situation is safe, take structural steps to modify their ongoing support system and prevent recurrence:
  • Contact NDIS Coordinator / LAC: Request an unscheduled, urgent "Change of Circumstances" plan review citing acute carer risk and physical danger.
  • Request Positive Behaviour Support: Request NDIS funding for a dedicated, accredited Behaviour Support Practitioner to draft an enforceable Positive Behaviour Support Plan (PBSP).
  • Incident Logbook: Document the date, exact time, antecedents/triggers, and violent actions of every escalation. This logging provides the empirical proof required to justify NDIS funding updates and medical reviews.
