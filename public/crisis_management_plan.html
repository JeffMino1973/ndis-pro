🛡️ Bronwyn's Crisis Protocol
1. Immediate Communication
  • Remain Calm: Consistently remind Bronwyn "Everything will be ok."
  • Cantonese Language Cue: If she is speaking in Cantonese, remind her to be calm and breathe in/out slowly.
  • Limit Input: Do not offer any other instructions while she is in an escalated state.
2. Assess Escalation

Assess Bronwyn's ability to calm herself. Once she demonstrates she is returning to a calm state, she can be gently reminded of the safety rules.

3. The "Calm Blue Chair" Rule

If hitting occurs at Home, the Shop, or in the Community:

  • At the Shop: Hitting is unacceptable and unsafe. She cannot remain at the shop. She must go to the calming blue chair in the park with a support person. Mum will call the support worker to assess safety before she can return.
  • At Home: Hitting is unacceptable and unsafe. She cannot remain at home. She must go to the calming blue chair in the park with a support person. Mum will call the support worker to assess safety before she can return.
  • In the Community: Hitting is unacceptable and unsafe. No activities. She must go to the calming blue chair in the park with a support person. Mum will call the support worker to assess safety before she can return to the home or shop.
